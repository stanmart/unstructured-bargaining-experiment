We will now discuss how the group is formed at the end of the round.

Group formation

During the bargaining stage, you can select which offer you currently prefer. The interface also displays the payoffs that would result from the current choices. At the end of the five minutes of discussion and bargaining, the currently accepted offer becomes final. Payoffs are then determined based on these final choices.

Once the round ends, the final outcome and the payoffs of this round are determined as follows: Only if all players in a proposed group agree on the same proposal is that proposal successful. Note, that players who are not included in a proposal do not have to agree to it. The group is then successfully formed and its members' payoffs are then determined by the agreed-upon proposal. All other players get 0. If there is no such agreement, all three players get 0.

Note that because Player 1 has to be included for a group to receive a budget, there will be one group at most.

Currently preferred proposal

During the five minutes of discussion and bargaining you can change your choice on which proposal you accept any number of times (see below for how the interface looks). At the end of the five minutes, everyone's currently accepted proposal becomes final.

This interface works very similarly to the "Choose a proposal" interface above. The main difference is that you can only make a choice for yourself (the choices of other players are fixed). (Just submit a new ID, or alternatively click "Revoke acceptance" if you do not wish to accept any of the proposals.)
