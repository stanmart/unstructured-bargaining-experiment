In this experiment you will play first a trial round (round 0) and then four regular rounds (round 1-4) of the following bargaining game:

The general structure:

You will play a bargaining game. In every round, you will play with four other participants. Each of you will be assigned a different player number ("Player 1/2/3/4/5"). Each bargaining round lasts exactly five minutes. After each bargaining round, we will reshuffle the participants and you will play with different participants than before. It will be done in such a way that you are unlikely to play with the same participants more than once. Player numbers will also be reshuffled. The reshuffling and your player number will not depend in any way on how you play.

The game:

Any combination of the five players can form a group. Each group generates some value (denoted in CHF). The value the group generates depends on its members:
  • If Player 1 is not in the group, the value is 0.
  • If Player 1 is in the group, the value is increasing in the number of members (see table and graph below for an example).
  • Only in round 4: The group value does not depend on whether Player 5 is in the group or not.

Note that the value each group generates can vary from round to round.

Every player has to two decisions to make: which players form a group and how the value of that group is allocated among its members. You can only submit positive integer values as allocations. At the end of the bargaining, each player submits their answer.

The payments of this round are then computed as follows: Only if all members of a proposed group agree on the same allocation will the bargaining be successful. The group members will then be paid according to the agreed-upon allocation, everyone else gets 0. Otherwise, everyone gets 0 in this round.

You will receive money at the end of the experiment. Your payment at the end of the experiment will consist of a participation fee of CHF 10 for showing up plus your payment randomly drawn from one of the (non-trial) rounds you played.
