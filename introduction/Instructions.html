In this experiment you will play first a trial round and then five regular rounds of the following bargaining game:

The general structure: You will bargain in groups of five. The game will be the same for each group. After each bargaining the round, the groups will be reshuffled. It will be done in such a way that you are unlikely to play with the same participants more than once. Each bargaining round will last five minutes.

The game: Any subset of players in each group can form a coalition. Each coalition generates some value (denoted in CHF). The value the coalition generates depends on its members:

  • If Player 1 is not in the coalition, the value is 0.
  • If Player 1 is in the coalition, the value is increasing in the number of members (see table and graph below for an example).
  • Only in round 5: The coalition value does not depend on whether Player 5 is in the coalition or not.

You will have two decisions to make: which players form a coalition and how the value of that coalition is allocated among its members. You can only submit positive integer values as allocations.

The bargaining interface: You will bargain over a text chat, you can do so in any way you like. There is only one group chat for everyone in the group, private chats are not possible. During the bargaining, players can make proposals in the proposal interface to help coordination ("Propose allocation"). They can propose as many proposals as they like. All proposals will be logged under "Past proposals". Players can indicate their currently most preferred proposal under "Preferred proposal", using the Proposal ID given in "Past proposals". Note that this is just for coordination purposes: "Preferred proposal" is not binding, it only indicates current preferences and can be changed at any time. At the end of the five minutes, a new page will open and you will have to enter which proposal you are choosing as your final answer (at this stage, communication will not be possible anymore). You will also have the possibility to reject all offers. The payoffs of that round will be based on those final answers.

The payment: You will receive a payment at the end of the experiment.

  • The payoffs in a given individual round is computed as follows: Only if all members of a proposed coalition agree on the same allocation, will the bargaining be successful and the value of that coalition paid out according to the agreed-upon allocation. Otherwise, everyone in the group gets 0 in this round.
  • Your final payoffs at the end of the experiment will then be computed as the show-up fee of CHF 10 plus your bargaining payoff randomly drawn from one of the (non-trial) rounds you played.
